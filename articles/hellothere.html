Since you're here, I don't want to waste your time (or mine by making huge efforts to make this nearly interesting), I present you the recipe of one of my favorite meal : the gratin dauphinois.

For this meal, you'll need: 1kg potatoes, 500ml milk or cream, 1 garlic clove, fresh thyme, grated nutmeg.

Rub some butter all over the surface of a gratin dish. Peel and slice the potatoes thin. Lay the slices on a clean tea towel and pat dry.

Pour the milk and cream into a saucepan. Add the garlic, thyme and nutmeg. Slowly heat the milk and, just as it is about to reach boiling point and you see bubbles appearing around the edge of the pan, remove it from the heat. Keep warm.

Layer the potato slices in the dish, slightly overlapping the slices and sprinkling with a little salt, freshly ground pepper and grated garlic between each layer.

Pour the hot milk and cream over the potatoes, then repeat the layering/pouring operation until the end. Scatter some cheese over the top and bake at medium temperature for about one hour. Leave the dish to stand for about 5 minutes, then serve sprinkled with a few fresh thyme leaves.
