Toy Story 2 (1999)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES

Production info(s) : The most animation produced in one week of production on Toy Story 2 was 5 minutes and 42 seconds.
The nearly 35,000 texture maps in Toy Story 2 occupy nearly 40 GB of disk space. There are more than 10,000 texture maps for the humans alone. It took over a month to digitally paint Al's car.

Monsters, Inc (2001)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES

Modeling info(s) : 90% of all the monsters in Monsters, Inc. have Mike's tongue.
Sulley has 25,336 "key" hairs, which are used to guide the motion and shape of the other 2.3 million hairs. A Technical Director hand animated the hairs on Sulley’s fingers to make them react to Boo's touch near the end of the movie.
All of the bedroom doors in the Door Vault in Monsters, Inc. were created from different combinations of 26 paint colors, 12 styles, 8 wood colors, 10 decals and 6 door knobs, and 3 hardware types.

Cars (2006)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES + Raytracing

Modeling info(s) : The opening race contained 60.000 seated cars and an additional 700 cars in the infield & aisles. The final race had 100,000 seated cars, plus an additional nearly 1500 cars in the infield and aisles. There are nearly 13,000 cars entering the Final Race stadium to find seats.

Rendering info(s) : There are a total of 21,566 lights in four different shots when Lighting McQueen and Mack first get on the road to California.

Monster House (2006)

Renderer : Sony's Arnold (branched from Autodesk's Arnold)

Rendering algorithm(s) : Path tracing (one bounce)

Production info(s) : The animation is also mostly based on performance capture.

Modeling info(s) : The hair of the characters are modeled with geometry, to mimic a stopmo look and to avoid building a hair system for path-tracing (an incredibly hard task resolved years later partly with the computer power increase).

Rendering info(s) : First feature-length animated movie using Monte Carlo path tracing. The images were rendered whithout motion blur or depth of field. DoF was added in compositing later on.

Ratatouille (2007)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES

Production info(s) : Pixar filmmakers created over 270 pieces of food in the computer. Every food item was prepared and styled in a real kitchen, then photographed for reference and eaten.
To save on time during Ratatouille, the artists at Pixar skipped one detail - none of the human characters have any toes.

WALL-E (2008)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES

Production info(s) : There are roughly 1000 unique props in the interior of WALL•E's truck. It took a team of five modelers and five shading artists more than a month to create them all.

Animations info(s) : Use of the WETA's Massive tool for animating the crowd shots, especially at the end of the movie.

Brave (2011)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES

Production info(s) : No Pixar film before Brave has had a castle in it, so the DunBroch castle had to be designed from the ground up, inside and out. Everything from the architecture of the structure itself to the tapestries in the hall and the moss on the stone walls was designed and built as 3D models in the computer. The production team visited, researched and studied a number of castles for reference.
Merida has more than 1500 individually sculpted, curly red strands that generate about 111,700 total hairs.

Monsters University (2013)

Renderer : Pixar's Renderman

Rendering algorithm(s) : REYES

Rendering info(s) : First use of physically based lighting workflow at Pixar. The film took 100 million CPU hours to render, which is equivalent to 10,000 years for a single computer.
